Phonotactics

The syllable structure is (C)1V(C)2
For (C)1, all consonants are allowed. Similarly, for V, all vowels are allowed.
For (C)2, only the nasals, unvoiced plosives, approximants, l, ly and r are allowed.
Double consonants are allowed, where C2 is the same consonant as the next syllable's C1.

Orthography

Romanization

Vowels are written following their group (a, e, i, o, u), and take an acute accent ◌́ if it is an up vowel. Even using though the accent is preferred, the vowel may be doubled if using the accent is not possible.

Most consonants are written following their IPA symbol, except for the ones in this table.

Logography

Logograms also mostly represent individual words, but some morphemes that inflect verbs/adjectives will have two readings based on the vowel groups. Their default reading is the low one.

Styling: The characters are organized in square blocks. They consist entirely of straight (parallel to the X or Y axis) lines and diagonal lines, and compound lines. The order in which they are written does not really matter.

Forming characters: The characters can be formed in one of three ways:

  • Pictographic: A picture or a representation of the idea.
  • Ideographic: Pictures joined together to form another idea.
  • Phonetic: A semantic component, giving an idea of meaning, and a phonetic component, with a similar sound.

Alphabet

Some of the simpler or simplified logograms are used also as an alphabet, to write loanwords and such phonetically, written with a line under.
