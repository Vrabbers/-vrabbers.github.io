Koon Laang is a currently nameless conlang. It is also anything-else-less.

Vowels

There are two sets of vowels: High vowels and low vowels, divided into 5 groups (a, e, i, o, u). This is important because some particles harmonize.

Group a e i o u
High a ɛ i ɔ ɯ
Low ə~ɐ e ɪ o u
In romanized orthography, the high ones can be represented with an acute accent, or by double vowels. (/a/ -> <á> or <aa>)

Phonotactics

The syllable structure is (C)1V(C)2
For (C)1, all consonants are allowed. Similarly, for V, all vowels are allowed.
For (C)2, only the nasals, unvoiced plosives, approximants, l and r are allowed.
Double consonants are allowed, where C2 is the same consonant as the next syllable's C1.

Sound change patterns:

From To Where
(C)VC1  V(C) (C)V  C1V(C)
(C)VC1  jV(C) (C)VC2  C2V(C) C1 is either n, ŋ, k or l
C2 is the palatal in the same row as C1.
(C)VC1  C2V(C) (C)V  C1C2V(C) C2 is either j or w.
Lowercase letters are their specific IPA symbol. A space separates syllables.

In the romanization system, every syllable is divided with a space.
